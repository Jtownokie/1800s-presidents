Presidents of the 19th Century

Thomas Jefferson

Thomas Jefferson (April 13, 1743 - July 4, 1826) - Served as the third President of the United States from 1801 to 1809. During the American Revolution, Jefferson was one of the primary authors of The Declaration of Independence, He also served as the second Governor of Virginia. After the war, Jefferson served as the first U.S. Secretary of State, and eventually founded the Jeffersonian Republican Party. For more information, please visit Wikipedia

Abraham Lincoln

Abraham Lincoln (February 12, 1809 - April 15, 1865) - Served as the 16th President of the United States from 1861 until his assassination in 1865. Lincoln led the U.S. during one of its' greatest trials and tribulations, the American Civil War. Under his leadership:

  • Great Britain was kept out of the war
  • The slaves were freed
  • The Confederacy was defeated

Due to his skill as a statesman, Lincoln is consistently regarded as the greatest U.S. President. For more information, please visit Wikipedia

Ulysses S. Grant

Ulysses S. Grant (April 27, 1822 - July 23, 1885) - Served as the 18th President of the United States from 1869 to 1877. Grant led the Union Army to victory in the Civl War and served as U.S. Secretary of War. As a member of the Radical Republicans, Grant was a staunch advocate for strict Reconstruction, and fought for civil rights for freedmen. During his Presidency, Grant appointed many African-Americans and Jewish-Americans to positions of government, prosecuted the Ku Klux Klan, and supported the ratification of the 15th Amendment.
